# Core CRM and Sales 12.2 update

## Dashboard - Measure of Sale cycle

The field – Sale Cycle – that shows the number of days from when a sale is created until it is closed. The field is automatically calculated for all new deals, and all existing closed deals have been updated with accurate values. The field is available in the dashboard module, as a filter in search, and as a column in archives.

What does this mean for you?

For sales representatives:

* Gain better insight into your own sales process and how long it takes to close deals
* Identify which sales approaches lead to faster results
* Prioritize opportunities with a higher likelihood of closing quickly

For sales managers:

* Track trends in sales cycle length over time at both team and individual levels
* Identify bottlenecks in the sales process
* Compare performance across teams and segments
* Use these insights to improve forecasting and planning

This provides a more data-driven foundation to optimize sales efforts and increase efficiency across the organization.

![Dashboard with My average and my groups sale cycle -screenshot][img1]

There are two ready made tile templates in the Library, an easy start for you to set this up in your dashboards:

* My average sale cycle per sale type this and previous year
* My group's average sale cycle per sale type this and previous year

![Dashboard tile edit for sale cycle -screenshot][img2]

### Find - Sale cycle as criteria and column

The sale cycle criteria and column gives you an option to see how long the sale have been in the status Open. The Sale cycle field will show number of days, this means you can search between, above or below different numbers.

## E-mail and Document - Download the e-mail or document file

You can download the e-mail or document from any archive your see. The same goes for document dialog.

The ability to download saved documents is a feature many users have been requesting for a long time.
By downloading an email, you can open a stored message in your locally installed email client. As OML is being phased out, we believe this provides a practical solution for those who still prefer working with emails in their local Outlook application.
